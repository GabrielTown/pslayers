This is an interactive tutorial that will teach you three basics of Photoshop Layers: Visibility, Opacity, and Blend Options. Hover over or click underlined and colored words for hints on where to go and what to do.



Visibility.

To the right or below you'll find an image and Layers sidebar much like you would have in Photoshop. This particular image is made up of three layers.

Click the eye () to the left of each layer to adjust its visibility.

After playing with the visibility... you should have a good idea of the position (top, middle, bottom) of the three layers. Also, you might not always want to see every layer when editing in Photoshop. Visibility allows us to focus on one or two layers at a time.

*The More You Know: a checkered pattern means that this part of the picture is invisible. If you save the image as a PNG file the checkered pattern takes on the color of whatever background the image is on.

Opacity.

Now we have a different image and Layers sidebar. A couple of differences between this image and the previous one are: this image has only two layers and Layer 1 is selected. You can tell Layer 1 is selected because it is highlighted with a lighter gray.

Click on the Opacity section and a slider will become visible. Use the slider to adjust the opacity of Layer 1. Opacity is a great tool and is enhanced with the next section.

Opacity: 100%

Blend
Options.

Like the Opacity example there are only two layers in this example, and Layer 1 is selected once again. This means that in this example you are blending Layer 1 into Layer 0.

The blending options can be found in the sidebar by clicking on the word Normal. A dropdown menu will appear. Select different blending options and see how it effects the image.

In this example there were only 5 blending options but... in Photoshop you have a lot more options. By adjusting the opacity along with changing the blending options you can create some unique and beautiful designs.

*The More You Know:
• Inside Photoshop you can add layers by clicking the plus () at the bottom of the Layers sidebar
• You can also delete selected layers by clicking the trash () button
• Once you master these skills you should search for tutorials on layer masks and layer effects
• Along with mastering layers you should learn how to use the Object Selection Tool, Magic Wand Tool, and the Brush Tool
